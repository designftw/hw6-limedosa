Needfinding for Chat/IM Apps:

  • Interview Questions:
  • Interviewing:
  • Conclusions:

Directions:

Part 1: Interview questions

Create a list of at least six meaningful starting questions specific to your use case (i.e., not the basic demographic or education questions) that you will ask people in your interviews, taking the advice on needfinding into account

Part 2: Interviewing

Interview at least three people, and record your interviews.

Part 3: Key Insights

List of (5-10)most-important conclusions.

Interview Questions:

1. What is your favorite social media platform?

    If the participant responds with a less mainstream platform:
    • How did you begin using this platform?
    • What do you like about the platform?
        • • What would you change about the app?
    Ask all participants:
    • What do you dislike about the platform?
    • What do you like about the platform?
        • • What would you change about the app?
    • Do you like the design of the app? What aspect of the app's design is your favorite?

2. What is your favorite way to communicate with:

    • family?
    • friends?
    • coworkers?
    • significant other?
    • professors?
    • classmates?

3. What are the biggest challenges you face when texting, and how do you currently overcome them?

    E.g. :
    • People not remembering to reply ==> Having to text people again
    • People taking too long to answer ==> Having to text people again

4. How do you currently communicate with your classmates and friends about academic and non-academic matters?

5. Have you ever used a messaging app that was specifically designed for a specific group(e.g. an app designed for college students only)? If so, what did you like and dislike about it?

    • What did you like about the app?
    • What did you dislike about the app?
    • What would you change about the app?

6. What kind of features would you like to see in a messaging app designed for college students that would enhance your academic and social experience?
